Images, signs, commodities, representations, are not only imperceptible to, but have become reality. Objects, the transformation of raw material through real human labour, flurry by as numbers, as values, severing the hands of its makers, or denying the hands ever existed at all. Your mind wonders to an escape, like what you’ve experienced before. Years ago. Don’t you remember? I think I’ve found it.

Cute is soft. Passive. Squishy. Pliable. Squeezable. Infinitely forgiving. It greets you as its mother, begging for warmth. It’s doe-eyed, and submissive, it whimpers, “Please take care of me”. Perhaps we can exist in this shelter together, you think to yourself. Oh, to be a child again. To relish in a favourite blanket or teddy bear, perhaps tattered or grubby, completely innocent, completely untainted by monetary value. Simply soothing. Innocuous.
"BY THIS LOGIC, THE EPITOME OF THE CUTE WOULD BE AN UNDIFFERENTIATED BLOB OF SOFT DOUGHY MATTER."
1. Sianne Ngai, Our Aesthetic Categories: Zany, Cute, Interesting. [Harvard University Press, 2012], 64.
The cute commodity seems to take on a fantastic quality, akin to that of the Magic Puppy children’s books, where the pet of one lucky child suddenly converses to only them. So you stare at your pet, or as perhaps you do now, stare at your cute things, imagining a relationship, a connection, that if I just believe hard enough, the fantasy will be real. One, of course, animate, the other illusory in its aliveness. Or perhaps you think to your childhood imaginary friend, an imagination so powerful it supersedes reality. An in-built comfort.
